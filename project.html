For eight years of my life, I devoted myself to music. It was my passion and my escape. I poured my heart and soul into learning, practicing and creating. Looking back on those years, I am filled with a sense of nostalgia and pride. I cherish the memories of performing in front of crowds, of collaborating with other musicians, and of exploring new sounds and styles. I am grateful for the discipline and focus that music instilled in me, and for the beautiful music I was able to sing. Throughout my musical journey, I faced various challenges that hindered my growth as an artist. Unfortunately, some of these challenges included harassment and bullying. Though I no longer pursue music as a career, it will always hold a special place in my heart. The skills and experiences that I gained through my musical journey continue to shape who I am today, and I will always be grateful for the time that I spent devoted to this art form.

Art

Art became a source of solace and a means of expression and painting allowed me to channel my emotions, and helped me to cope with the loss of music. I discovered that the process of creating art had a calming effect on my mind and body. While I may not be a master painter, the act of putting colors on a canvas or sketching out a design allowed me to lose myself in the moment and forget about everything else. Art has been a vital part of my healing journey and has given me a new form of creative expression to explore.
