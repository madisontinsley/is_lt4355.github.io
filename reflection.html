My Journey

My mother says I sang before I could talk. While that could be an exaggeration, I do know I have been singing for a long time. My parents have countless videos of me over the years, singing with a CD player covered in pink and purple flowers; singing every single word of the 2004 movie adaptation of the Phantom of the Opera; singing solos in front of judges in a long black dress. I feel as though I create life when I sing, and I followed music like a moth to the flame.

I mentioned on the home page of this website that I find it difficult to recognize myself form two years ago, this was when I was a freshmen in college at Pepperdine University. I was an aspiring opera singer, going to a reputable music school and trying desperately not to complain about having an entire year online. I realize now that I was clinging to music as a way to avoid something that scares me: I had no idea who I was. Compared to now, where I only know what I am not, but there are no limits as to who I am.

My decision to quit music became from reasons I never thought of. The music department at Pepperdine University prides itself on being a “family” a concept that sounds good on paper, but in reality creates a toxic environment that few can grow in. I got the brunt of this toxicity as soon as I was labelled the outcast. Music from that point forward became miserable for me, and it took me almost six months to begin singing. Once I quit music, I realized that my voice is the mostprecious posession to me. 1I have to decide when I share it, why I share it and that I enjoy sharing it.

Now, I have found more passions than just music. I have found a love for painting, art history, and most importantly, coding and technology. I grew up mostly an only child, and with this, a love for video games, as I only had myself to entertain. As I sat down to decide what was next after Pepperdine, I decided to dissect everything I have ever loved, seeing if any sparks turned into more. Technology was that for me, something that is much more creative than meets the eye.
